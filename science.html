The Science — Lightning, Tamed

Cold atmospheric plasma generated via dielectric barrier discharge (DBD) is a well-established, safe technology already used in medicine, food processing, and surface sterilization. DBD is widely recognized in the peer-reviewed literature as the most proven and scalable configuration for agricultural applications because it operates at atmospheric pressure and near-ambient temperature while producing controlled reactive species without thermal damage.

When applied to soil it produces a precise cocktail of electrons, UV photons, and reactive oxygen/nitrogen species that:

  • Inactivate bacteria, fungi, and other pathogens (Misra et al., 2019)
  • Degrade a wide range of pesticide molecules into less toxic or inert compounds (Gavahian et al., 2021; Sojithamporn et al., 2023)
  • Fix atmospheric nitrogen into nitrates, nitrites, and other plant-available forms — essentially creating a gentle, on-site fertilizer (Lamichhane et al., 2021)
  • Stimulate beneficial microbial activity and plant defense responses

The foundational safety and efficacy of low-temperature plasma for biological applications was established in seminal work by Laroussi (2005). Recent reviews confirm its broad potential for sustainable agriculture and soil remediation (Pańka et al., 2022).

It is chemical-free, low-energy, residue-free, and fast — exactly what both Earth’s stressed soils and humanity’s off-world ambitions require.
